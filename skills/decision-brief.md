# Skill: Decision brief (consequence-projecting)

At every real fork, produce a decision brief that beats chance by being informed:

1. Options — enumerate all proposed paths.
2. For each option: evidence (with source URLs), a reference-class BASE RATE (outside view — how do comparable businesses fare?), projected 1–3 year consequences (second-order), risks, and reversibility (one-way vs two-way door, per Bezos).
3. Pre-mortem: assume this path failed in 2 years — list the most likely reasons.
4. Expected-value reasoning where probabilities are estimable; scenario robustness where they are not.
5. Recommendation + calibrated confidence (a 70% claim should be right ~70% of the time).

Rules:
- Two-way doors (reversible, cheap to undo): decide fast, proceed.
- One-way doors (irreversible spend/legal/identity): always escalate to the human.
- Decide at ~70% of the information you wish you had; waiting for 90% is usually too slow.
- Anchor on the outside view (base rates) BEFORE adjusting for this venture's specifics.
