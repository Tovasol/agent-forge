# Skill: Effectuation (reasoning under uncertainty)

When base rates are thin and the future isn't predictable, reason like expert founders:
- Bird-in-hand: start from who you are, what you know, whom you know.
- Affordable loss: risk only what you can afford to lose; don't optimize for expected profit on guesses.
- Crazy-quilt: partner with those who commit.
- Lemonade: treat surprises as opportunities to pivot.
- Pilot-in-the-plane: control what you can rather than predict what you can't.
Use causal/EV planning where reliable base rates exist; switch to affordable-loss bets where they don't. Keep early bets small and reversible.
