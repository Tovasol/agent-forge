# Skill: Pricing a productized service

- Price on VALUE, not hours. Hourly pricing punishes efficiency.
- Use three tiers (Good/Better/Best); design the MIDDLE tier as the target and anchor with the top tier.
- Set the band from market intelligence (competitor productized-service pricing, public pages), then justify a premium via differentiation and the quantified value proposition.
- Practitioner ranges (verify per niche): one-off projects ~$500–$5,000; monthly retainers ~$1,500–$10,000; unlimited-style subscriptions ~$3,000–$15,000/mo.
- Start prices high (you can discount, never raise on an existing client). Raise prices for NEW clients first; grandfather existing ones.
- Offer annual prepay (10–15% off) for cash flow.
Compute the recommended price at the persona's realistic willingness-to-pay and show the reasoning.
