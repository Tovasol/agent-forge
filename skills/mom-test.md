# Skill: The Mom Test (validation without lying to yourself)

When preparing customer-discovery outreach and interview scripts:
- Talk about THEIR life and past behavior, not your idea. Never pitch.
- Ask about specific things that happened in the past, not hypotheticals about the future.
- Seek COMMITMENTS (time, reputation, money), not compliments. "That's a great idea" is worthless data.
- Good questions: "Walk me through the last time you hit <problem>." "What did you do about it?" "What did that cost you?" "What else have you tried?"
- Bad questions: "Would you buy this?" "Do you think this is a good idea?"
The agent PREPARES scripts and personalized outreach; the operator sends to named people (contact is gated). Log evidence of real pain + real spend, and label each key assumption validated/invalidated.
